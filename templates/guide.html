Vaccine Information


This is a reference for vaccine which contains information about different types of vaccines.Vaccines typically involve multiple doses to ensure adequate immune response, often administered in a primary series followed by booster shots. Primary doses initiate the immune system's recognition of the pathogen, while boosters enhance and prolong immunity over time. Timing and intervals between doses are crucial for optimizing vaccine efficacy and achieving robust and lasting protection against infectious diseases.


Influenza


Injection Site: deltoid muscle of the upper arm.

Dosage: 0.5 milliliters

Booster: Single dose

Age: 6 months and older

MenACWY


Injection Site: Thigh

Dosage: 0.5 milliliters

Booster: 16 years old

Age: 11-12 years old

MenB(meningococcal B)


Injection Site: deltoid muscle of the upper arm.

Dosage: Multiple

Booster: Multiple

Age: adolescents and young adults

RSV


Injection Site: intramuscular injection.

Dosage: 2

Booster: At certain high-risk groups or individuals.

Age: infants and young children

MenABCWY(meningococcal serogroups A, B, C, W, and Y)


Injection Site: intramuscular injection, commonly in the upper arm.

Dosage: multiple

Booster: multiple

Age: adolescents and young adults

HPV(Human Papillomavirus Vaccination):


Injection Site: upper arm or thigh.

Dosage: single dose

Booster: two or three doses after single dose

Age: 11 or 12 years old

Tdap (tetanus, diphtheria, and pertussis (whooping cough))


Injection Site: deltoid muscle of the upper arm.

Dosage: initially 1

Booster: every 10 years after the initial dose.

Age: during pregnancy usually 27 and 36 weeks of gestation.

DTP (Diphtheria, Tetanus, Pertussis)


Injection Site: Infants: Thigh muscle Older Children and Adults: Upper arm muscle (deltoid)

Dosage: 2

Booster: during adolescence and adulthood

Age: 11-12 years.

Polio


Injection Site: deltoid muscle of the upper arm for adults.

Dosage: 2

Booster: 10 years

Age: 2 months, 4 months, 6-18 months, and 4-6 years old.

Hepatitis-B


Injection Site: deltoid muscle of the upper arm or the anterolateral thigh muscle.

Dosage: 2

Booster: 5, 10, or 20 years

Age: any age, from infants to older adults

Rotavirus


Injection Site: administered orally, as drops

Dosage: 2

Booster: not typically needed during childhood.

Age: around 2 months of age

Varicella


Injection Site: injection into the muscle, often in the upper arm (deltoid muscle) for adults and older children, or in the thigh for infants and younger children.

Dosage: single-dose injection

Booster: second dose is given at 4-6 years of age

Age: 12-15 months of age

MMR


Injection Site: injection at the upper arm or thigh

Dosage: 0.5 mL

Booster: second dose is given at 4-6 years of age

Age: around 12-15 months of age
