Immunization Importance 101


Vaccination plays a crucial role in protecting children from various infectious diseases.


Prevention of Diseases

Vaccines help prevent potentially serious illnesses such as measles, mumps, rubella, polio, whooping cough, hepatitis, influenza, and significantly reduce their risk of contracting these diseases.

Protection of Community Health

Vaccination not only protects the vaccinated child but also helps prevent the spread of diseases within the community.

Protection Against Future Health Risks

Vaccines not only protect children from current threats but also guard against potential health risks. By vaccinating against diseases like HPV children can reduce their risk of developing cancers later in life.

Reduced Severity of Illness

Vaccines can help mitigate symptoms and prevent complications, leading to shorter recovery times and fewer hospitalizations.
